# Final Recommendation

## Key Findings

The regression analysis identified three variables that are strongly associated with monthly sales performance:

1. Footfall
2. Marketing Spend
3. Inventory Availability Percentage

Among these factors, footfall has the strongest relationship with sales and explains a large proportion of sales variation.

## Variables Leadership Should Focus On

### Footfall

Stores with higher customer traffic consistently generate higher sales. Leadership should invest in initiatives that increase customer visits, including promotions, local marketing campaigns, and customer engagement programs.

### Marketing Spend

Marketing spend has a positive and statistically significant relationship with sales. Targeted marketing investments should continue to support demand generation.

### Inventory Availability

Higher inventory availability is associated with higher sales. Maintaining product availability and reducing stock-outs should remain a priority.

## Variables That Should Not Be Over-Interpreted

The Region North dummy variable was not statistically significant in the final model. This suggests that regional differences alone do not strongly explain sales performance after accounting for operational factors.

## Recommended Business Actions

* Increase customer footfall through targeted campaigns.
* Maintain high inventory availability levels.
* Continue optimizing marketing spend.
* Monitor store performance using operational KPIs.
* Investigate stores with unusually large residuals for additional business insights.

## Risks and Limitations

* Regression analysis identifies relationships but does not prove causation.
* External factors such as economic conditions, competitor actions, and local market dynamics may influence sales.
* Future performance may differ from historical patterns.

## Conclusion

The final multiple regression model explains approximately 80.5% of the variation in monthly sales. The strongest business drivers are footfall, marketing spend, and inventory availability. Leadership should focus on these factors when developing growth strategies and operational improvement plans.
